### **5 Hard-Hitting Discovery Questions**
*(Designed to uncover operational realities, test their claims, or introduce competitive doubt)*

1.  **The Bandwidth/Resource Question:** 
    *"You highlight an impressive track record of over 265 delivered projects but specifically mention 26 satisfied clients [3, 5]. Does this mean your team's bandwidth is heavily tied up in a few legacy accounts, and how can you guarantee dedicated, agile support for a new account?"*
2.  **The Generalist vs. Specialist Question:** 
    *"You offer a massive, full-service suite—from web development and paid traffic to in-house podcast production and photography [3]. With your current 100% female team [1], how many of these highly specialized tasks are handled in-house versus being outsourced to third-party freelancers?"*
3.  **The Overhead Question:** 
    *"It’s great that you recently opened a new physical office and production studio in Santo André [2, 4]. However, if a client only requires strictly digital performance services like Paid Traffic and SEO [10], how do you ensure their retainer isn't artificially inflated to subsidize the overhead of your physical studio?"*
4.  **The Attribution Question:** 
    *"You proudly claim to have generated over 1.5 million in sales for your clients [3]. In an omnichannel environment where you handle everything from branding to podcasts [6], what exact attribution models do you use to prove that those sales were a direct result of your campaigns rather than the client's organic momentum?"*
5.  **The Methodology Pivot Question:** 
    *"Your 4-step methodology moves smoothly from 'Strategy' to 'Success' [7, 8]. However, marketing is rarely a straight line. Can you walk me through a recent example where the 'Execution' phase failed to hit its KPIs, and exactly how your team pivoted to protect the client's ROI?"*
